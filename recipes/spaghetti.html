Description

A plate of perfectly al dente spaghetti, cooked to tender perfection. Each strand is coated in a rich, velvety tomato sauce, simmered to a harmonious blend of flavors. The aroma alone, a tantalizing mix of garlic, basil, and ripe tomatoes, beckons you to take a bite. But it doesn't stop there ~ a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese adds a creamy, salty kick, while a handful of fragrant, torn basil leaves brings a burst of freshness to the dish.

This well-prepared spaghetti is a symphony of taste and texture, a testament to a decade of culinary expertise. With every forkful, you'll savor the love and dedication that went into creating this Italian masterpiece.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti pasta
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic cloves
  • red pepper flakes (for some heat, optional)
  • crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Italian seasoning blend (or a mix of dried oregano, basil, and thyme)
  • Red wine (optional, for added depth of flavor)
  • Sugar (optional, to balance the acidity of the tomatoes)

These ingredients will help you create a flavorful and well-seasoned spaghetti sauce that's sure to impress your taste buds. Enjoy!

Steps

  1. Boil Spaghetti: Cook spaghetti pasta in a large pot of salted boiling water according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté Garlic and Red Pepper (Optional): In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and red pepper flakes (if using). Sauté for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add Crushed Tomatoes or Sauce: Pour in crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce into the skillet. Stir and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken. If desired, add a splash of red wine for extra flavor.
  4. Season the Sauce: Season the sauce with salt, black pepper, and the Italian seasoning blend (or a mix of dried oregano, basil, and thyme). Adjust seasoning to taste. If the sauce is too acidic, you can add a pinch of sugar to balance the flavors.
  5. Combine with Pasta: Add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet with the sauce. Toss to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce.
  6. Add Fresh Basil: Tear or chop fresh basil leaves and sprinkle them over the spaghetti. Gently mix them into the dish.
  7. Serve: Plate the spaghetti, garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese, and serve hot.

Enjoy your delicious homemade spaghetti!
